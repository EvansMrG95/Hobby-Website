Common Freshwater Fish Species

Roach

Roach are small, silver-scaled freshwater fish commonly found in rivers, lakes, and canals across the UK. They are easily recognised by their reddish fins and rounded body. Roach are social fish that often swim in shoals and feed on insects, plants, and small invertebrates. They're a popular target in coarse fishing due to their abundance, willingness to bite, and the light tackle challenge they provide.

Perch

Perch are often an easy catch as they are a predator which will eat anything that moves. Once caught it is recommended to run your hand down from the head over the gills and dorsal fin due to their harmless spikes.

Carp

Carp are a very popular fish, the larger fish are targeted specifically with larger baits and heaver tackle. They come in different sub-species of Grass Carp, Mirror Carp, Crucian Carp, and Ghost Carp

Bream

Bream are deep-bodied, flat-sided freshwater fish commonly found in slow-moving rivers, lakes, and canals across the UK. They have a bronze or golden sheen and long, curved dorsal fins. Bream often feed on the bottom, sifting through silt for worms, insects, and plant matter. Known for their shoaling nature and steady resistance when hooked, they are a favourite among coarse anglers, especially during the warmer months.

Rudd

Rudd are often found in large shouls and can be easy to catch with smaller baits. Their colour can often reflect on the water quality and habitat conditions.

Chub

Chub are robust, torpedo-shaped freshwater fish found in rivers and streams throughout the UK. They have a dark back, silvery sides, and distinct white lips, with fins often tinged orange. Chub are opportunistic feeders, eating insects, small fish, and even berries or bread. Pike

Pike are agressive predators with teeth, they are best handled by the lips under the mouth. Unhooking is best done with forceps or gloves.

Tench

Tench are a personal favourite of mine and are often referred to as a "bar of soap". They are very soft and slimey, they are bottom feeders and prefer quieter waters.
